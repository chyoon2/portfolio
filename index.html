About Me:

I grew up in Mill Creek, WA a tight knit community north of Seattle. After college I started my professional career in the healthcare field. During my time as an Office Assistant then Office Manager at Eyes of Seattle, Most importantly, I learned to provide excellent patient care and worked to overcome the challenges in the healthcare system to get people the services they needed. As an outgoing kid, I was lucky to catch the theater bug early. Now as a teacher and producer at the Pocket Theater, I derive most of my enjoyment from helping  artists come together and leading them on paths to success. Theater is where I  learned to work both happily and efficiently in a fast-paced, busy environment. I’ve  listened to students and artists and developed systems to meet their needs and  requests; I created and maintained an open, professional culture emphasizing  community. Community and creative expression are aspects of our health that often go overlooked.
